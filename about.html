Brief History of Akintan

Akintan Senior Grammar School was founded in October 1982 from the defunct Surulere grammar school, established by Domingos who also served as the pioneer Principal. The present School, Akintan Senior Grammar School, came into being on 6th January 2003, followsing the splitting of Akintan Grammar School into Junior and Senior Categories


School Motto
The phrase "Per Crucem ad Lucem" is a Latin motto that translates to "Through the Cross to the Light" in English.
"Per Crucem" means "Through the Cross," symbolizing overcoming challenges, sacrifices, or hardships.
"Ad Lucem" translates to "To the Light," representing the attainment of enlightenment, success, or a better state after enduring trials.
Overall, the phrase conveys the idea that one must endure and persevere through difficulties in order to reach a place of growth, understanding, or achievement


Dress Code

The dress code at Akintan Senior Grammar School is not merely a matter of attire; it's a symbol of our identity and values. The elegant combination of white and darkblue embodies the essence of our community, weaving together layers of meaning that resonate with our students, faculty, and alumni.

White:
With its pristine hue, symbolizes purity and clarity of purpose. It signifies the school’s focus on maintaining a positive learning environment and fostering values of honesty and respect among students.

Darkblue:
Complementing the purity of white is the rich, deep tone of blue. This color represents discipline, strength, and stability. It conveys a sense of professionalism and responsibility, reflecting the school’s commitment to nurturing well-rounded students.
